# Open Questions for Exploration

## Phase 1 Extensions

### 1. Wealth Mobility
Track the top 10% wealthiest agents over time. Do they stay at the top, or does the system constantly reshuffle ranks? This probes whether inequality is "positional" (fixed ranks, changing people) or "structural" (locked-in advantage).

### 2. Ergodicity
Does a single agent's time-averaged wealth equal the ensemble average across all agents at one snapshot? If not, GDP growth doesn't imply individual benefit — a deep connection to the ergodicity economics debate (Ole Peters, 2019).

### 3. Taxation and Redistribution
Introduce a flat tax Event (e.g., 1% per tick, redistributed equally). What is the equilibrium Gini as a function of tax rate? Is the relationship linear, convex, or concave?

### 4. Multiplicative vs Additive Exchange
Phase 1 uses additive exchange (fixed amount). Switching to proportional exchange (fraction of wealth) should shift the distribution from exponential to **power-law** (Pareto tail). This is the key mechanism that pushes real-world Gini from 0.5 toward 0.7+.

### 5. Memory of Initial Conditions
Start with extreme inequality (1 agent holds 90% of total wealth). Does the system still converge to the same exponential distribution? How long does it take — and is the convergence time related to the initial Gini?

## Future Phases

### 6. Absorbing Wall Dynamics
With bankruptcy (wealth=0 → permanent exit), the system evolves toward winner-take-all. How does elimination rate depend on delta/mean_wealth ratio? What is the survival curve shape?

### 7. Multi-Asset Economy
Introduce consumable goods (grain) alongside durable money (coin). Agents must consume grain to survive, creating demand asymmetry. Do prices emerge endogenously from subjective valuations?

### 8. Strategy and Game Theory
Replace random exchange with Prisoner's Dilemma. Introduce strategies (Always Cooperate, Always Defect, Tit-for-Tat). Under evolutionary pressure (reproduction proportional to wealth), which strategies dominate?

### 9. Network and Geography
Replace random pairing with network-based or geography-based matching. How does network topology (small-world, scale-free, lattice) affect wealth distribution and Gini?

### 10. Institutions and External Shocks
Model governments (taxation, redistribution), central banks (money supply), natural disasters (wealth destruction in a region), and wars (forced adversarial interaction between groups). How do these events alter the equilibrium distribution?
